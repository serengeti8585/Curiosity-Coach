The problem

AI can become an answer machine.

When students use AI only to get answers, they can skip the most important part of learning: the struggle to observe, reason, test ideas, and explain evidence.

Curiosity Coach starts from a different question: how can AI slow students down enough to make their thinking visible?

  • Guided inquiry before answers
  • Reasoning and evidence before conclusions
  • Student thinking captured through interaction history

The Curiosity Coach approach

From “What is the answer?” to “How did you think?”

01

Observe

Students begin with what they can see, notice, or measure.

02

Question

They turn observations into better scientific questions.

03

Infer

They connect clues to possible explanations.

04

Hypothesize

They predict what could happen and why.

05

Revise

They adjust ideas when new evidence appears.

06

Explain

They build a final explanation using evidence and reasoning.

The interaction history becomes evidence of learning.

Each student response shows what they noticed, what they misunderstood, how their reasoning changed, and whether they can connect evidence to a conclusion.

For teachers

Use Curiosity Coach after instruction.

Teachers can use CC after direct instruction, videos, slides, readings, labs, or demonstrations. The teacher provides the learning focus, and CC guides students through structured thinking.

Because the guide asks questions instead of simply giving answers, it can support ESE, ELL, standard, and advanced students with different levels of scaffolding.

For administrators

Controlled, teacher-directed AI use.

Curiosity Coach is designed as a teacher-directed prototype, not an open-ended student chatbot. It focuses on AI literacy, critical thinking, source evaluation, reasoning, and measurable evidence of student thinking.

The goal is not to replace teachers or student effort. The goal is to make the learning process easier to see, guide, and discuss.
