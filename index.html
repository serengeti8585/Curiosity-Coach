The problem

AI can become an answer machine.

When students use AI only to get answers, they can skip the most important part of learning: the struggle to observe, reason, test ideas, and explain evidence.

Curiosity Coach starts from a different question: how can AI slow students down enough to make their thinking visible?

  • Guided inquiry before answers
  • Reasoning and evidence before conclusions
  • Student thinking captured through interaction history

The Curiosity Coach approach

From “What is the answer?” to “How did you think?”

01

Observe

Students begin with what they can see, notice, or measure.

02

Question

They turn observations into better scientific questions.

03

Infer

They connect clues to possible explanations.

04

Hypothesize

They predict what could happen and why.

05

Revise

They adjust ideas when new evidence appears.

06

Explain

They build a final explanation using evidence and reasoning.

The interaction history becomes evidence of learning.

Each student response shows what they noticed, what they misunderstood, how their reasoning changed, and whether they can connect evidence to a conclusion.

Prototype transparency

This interactive prototype demonstrates the instructional framework behind Curiosity Coach. It does not use a Large Language Model. Instead, it uses a scripted decision tree to show how students can be guided through observation, questioning, inference, hypothesis, revision, and explanation.

Because this prototype follows predefined pathways rather than understanding natural language, visitors may occasionally need to rephrase or clarify an answer. Curiosity Coach uses an LLM to understand the meaning behind student responses, adapt its coaching in real time, and personalize guidance while remaining under teacher control.

The purpose of this prototype is to demonstrate the instructional design — not the AI itself.

For teachers

Teacher-controlled learning experiences.

Teachers determine the content, focus, standards, images, and instructional goals. Curiosity Coach does not replace the teacher or curriculum; it guides students through teacher-selected learning experiences.

Teachers can use CC after direct instruction, videos, slides, readings, labs, or demonstrations. The teacher provides the learning focus, and CC guides students through structured thinking that keeps evidence and reasoning visible.

  • Teacher-selected standards and learning targets
  • Teacher-approved sources, images, and prompts
  • Guidance that supports—not replaces—instruction

Differentiated instruction

The same lesson can meet different learners where they are.

AP students

Receive deeper reasoning prompts that ask them to evaluate mechanisms, qualify claims, and connect evidence across steps.

Standard students

Receive guided evidence-based questions that help them explain why an answer makes sense instead of stopping at a conclusion.

ESE and ELL students

Receive more scaffolding, sentence starters, vocabulary support, and step-by-step guidance while working toward the same instructional goal.

Across subjects

Curiosity Coach can guide inquiry beyond one topic.

Science

Students investigate marine iguanas by observing evidence, forming hypotheses, and explaining adaptation.

Algebra

Students explain why each step in solving an equation works instead of only getting the answer.

History

Students examine primary sources, identify perspective or bias, and build evidence-based historical claims.

ELA

Students analyze a passage, support interpretation with textual evidence, and revise claims.

For administrators

Controlled, teacher-directed AI use.

Curiosity Coach is designed as a teacher-directed prototype, not an open-ended student chatbot. It focuses on AI literacy, critical thinking, source evaluation, reasoning, and measurable evidence of student thinking.

The goal is not to replace teachers or student effort. The goal is to make the learning process easier to see, guide, and discuss.
